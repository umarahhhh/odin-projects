Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a rich, comforting Italian dish known for its layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and gooey melted mozzarella.

Baked to perfection, each bite delivers a harmonious blend of flavors and textures, with the pasta providing a satisfying chew, the sauce offering a robust, herby taste, and the cheese adding a luscious, creamy finish.

This hearty casserole is often enjoyed as a family meal, embodying both simplicity and indulgence in every delicious layer.

Ingredients

  • Pasta sheets
  • Sauce
  • Cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
  2. Cook the lasagna according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Make the meat sauce: In a skillet, cook ground meat (until browned).
  4. Stir in sauce, salt, pepper, and any additional herbs or spices.
  5. Simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes, then remove from heat.
  6. Spread a thing layer of sauce on the bottom of the baking dish.
  7. Place a layer of lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  8. Spread a portion of cheese over the noodles.
  9. Repeat the layers (sauce, noodles, cheese) until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of sauce and cheese on top.
  10. Bake in the oven for 25-30 minutes.
  11. Let the lasagna cool for about 10-15 minutes before slicing.
